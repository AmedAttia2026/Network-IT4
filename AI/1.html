Part 2 – Short-Answer Questions (Model Answers)

1. Define Artificial Intelligence in your own words.
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of computer science that aims to create machines capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as reasoning, learning, and problem-solving.
2. What is the main difference between AI, Data Science, and Analytics?
AI focuses on building systems that can make decisions and learn automatically; Data Science focuses on extracting insights from data; Analytics emphasizes interpreting data to support decision-making.
3. Give one example of a real-world AI application you use in daily life.
Examples: voice assistants (Siri, Alexa), recommendation systems (Netflix, YouTube), facial recognition on smartphones, chatbots, or self-driving cars.
4. State one ethical concern that arises with the increasing use of AI.
Bias and fairness, privacy violations, job displacement, or lack of transparency in automated decision-making.
5. Why is the data-driven paradigm considered essential for modern AI systems?
Because modern AI models—especially machine learning and deep learning—depend on large amounts of data to learn patterns and make accurate predictions or decisions.

Part 3 – Open-Ended Discussion Questions (Suggested Key Points)

Click the button to reveal suggested key points for discussion.

1. Compare and contrast Classical AI, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning. Which is most impactful today, and why?
Classical AI used rule-based logic and symbolic reasoning; Machine Learning allows systems to learn from data; Deep Learning uses neural networks to model complex patterns. Deep Learning is currently the most impactful due to its success in image and speech recognition, and natural language processing.
2. How did advances in data availability and computing power contribute to the resurgence of AI?
The availability of big data and powerful GPUs allowed AI systems to train on massive datasets, improving their performance and reviving interest in machine learning and deep learning after the AI Winter.
3. In your opinion, which industry will be most transformed by AI in the next decade? Provide reasons.
Possible industries: healthcare (diagnosis automation), transportation (self-driving cars), finance (fraud detection), or education (personalized learning). Students should justify their choice based on societal impact or technological feasibility.
4. Do you think AI will create more jobs than it replaces, or vice versa? Support your answer with examples.
AI may replace routine and repetitive jobs but create new roles in AI maintenance, data analysis, and ethics oversight. The overall effect depends on how societies adapt through retraining and education.
5. Looking at the future of AI, do you believe it poses more opportunities or risks for society? Why?
Balanced answer expected: opportunities include efficiency and innovation; risks involve ethical misuse, unemployment, and bias. The impact depends on governance, education, and responsible AI development.
